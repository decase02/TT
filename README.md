# Charter Code of the Central American Trading Company

**Amended and Restated — Revision 3**  
**A Private Trading, Infrastructure, Digital-Governance, and Territorial Administration Services Company**

---

## Preamble

The Central American Trading Company is established as a private trading, infrastructure, digital-governance, and territorial-administration services company.

The Company exists to provide lawful, voluntary, contract-based governance services to private communities, special jurisdictions, economic zones, charter settlements, digital communities, and commercial enclaves.

The Company does not claim independent sovereignty, statehood, diplomatic status, military authority, criminal jurisdiction, or the power to override the laws of any host state. Its authority is private, contractual, proprietary, administrative, and delegated only where recognized by applicable law.

The Company is inspired by historic chartered trading companies only in the sense of combining commerce, infrastructure, administration, logistics, law, and governance services. It expressly rejects conquest, forced rule, extraction, slavery, coercive monopolies, private warfare, and involuntary subjection.

The Company binds itself to this Code not because external power compels it, but because durable commerce requires that the Company's promises be worth more than its convenience.

# Book I — Foundational Principles

## Article 1 — Nature of the Company

The Company is a private legal entity engaged in:

1. trade and logistics;
2. digital infrastructure;
3. lawful crypto and digital-asset services;
4. private registry services;
5. arbitration and dispute-administration support;
6. territorial management services;
7. infrastructure development;
8. community governance administration;
9. compliance, identity, and resident-services platforms.

The Company may operate physical enclaves, digital jurisdictions, commercial platforms, research zones, private communities, ports, warehouses, marketplaces, or governance-service territories only under lawful title, lease, contract, concession, license, or delegation.

The Company shall remain privately held unless the Governor expressly authorizes otherwise through a formal amendment to this Code.

## Article 2 — Non-Sovereignty Clause

The Company is not a state.

The Company shall not claim:

1. independent diplomatic recognition;
2. national sovereignty;
3. criminal jurisdiction beyond what is delegated by law;
4. authority to wage war;
5. authority to imprison or detain persons beyond what host-state law expressly permits;
6. authority to collect taxes except lawful service fees, assessments, rents, dues, or delegated public charges;
7. authority to exclude lawful public authorities from exercising their jurisdiction.

Any territorial governance performed by the Company shall be framed as governance services, administrative management, private-community regulation, or delegated special-zone administration, not sovereign rule.

Where any provision of this Code is capable of two readings, the reading consistent with this Article prevails.

## Article 3 — Rule of Law

All Company territories and digital communities shall be governed by written rules.

No person may be punished, expelled, fined, suspended, or deprived of access except under published rules or emergency procedures authorized by this Code.

Emergency powers shall be reviewable under Article 13.

Residents, users, merchants, contractors, and Villagers shall have access to a dispute-resolution process meeting the requirements of Book VII.

## Article 3A — Hierarchy of Law

Host-state law overrides this Code in all cases. This Code operates only within the space that applicable law leaves to private ordering.

This Code overrides all subordinate Company instruments, including local charters, platform rules, merchant regulations, and treasury rules.

A contract between the Company and a Resident, Villager, Chartered Merchant, or client may grant rights greater than this Code provides, but may not reduce the protections of the Entrenched Articles.

# Book II — Status, Membership, Residency, and Property License

## Article 4 — Classes of Persons

The Company may recognize the following classes:

**Governor** — the supreme executive and interpretive authority of the Company and this Code.

**Legal Representative** — the legally registered representative with binding signatory authority.

**Director** — a senior officer appointed to administer a department or territory.

**Chartered Merchant** — a business or individual authorized to trade under Company rules.

**Resident** — a person lawfully permitted to live within a Company-administered territory.

**Villager** — a member of the Company's digital or physical community platform who is not a Resident.

**Visitor** — a temporary guest with limited access rights.

**Contractor** — a person or entity providing services under contract.

**Credentialed Observer** — a journalist, researcher, auditor, or monitor granted defined transparency access.

**Third Party** — any person interacting with the Company without membership, residency, or contractual status.

## Article 4A — Credentialed Observers

The Company may credential Observers for defined purposes, durations, and areas.

Observer credentials shall state in writing:

1. the access granted;
2. the access excluded;
3. any confidentiality obligations;
4. the revocation procedure.

Observer access may be conditioned on safety, security, and privacy rules, but credentials shall not be revoked solely for lawful unfavorable reporting.

Revocation for cause is subject to written reasons and review.

## Article 5 — Digital-to-Physical Parity

Digital identity shall serve as the primary administrative identity within Company systems.

Physical access to Company-administered territory may be tied to digital status, subject to the limits in this Article.

Suspension of digital access may trigger restriction of physical access to commercial, platform, and non-essential facilities only.

### Dwelling and Essential-Services Guarantee

No suspension, emergency directive, fee dispute, rule violation, or other Company action shall interrupt, condition, or deny:

1. a Resident's access to their dwelling;
2. essential utilities serving that dwelling, including power, water, sanitation, and any utility legally required;
3. lawful means of entry to and exit from the territory.

Removal of a Resident from a dwelling may occur only through the legal process of the host jurisdiction.

Emergency digital suspension may occur immediately where there is a credible threat to safety, systems, infrastructure, property, legal compliance, or public order, subject to review and the Dwelling and Essential-Services Guarantee.

## Article 5B — Company Property and Tokenized Licenses

### 1. Company Proprietary Ownership

All land, buildings, infrastructure, utilities, common areas, registries, access systems, administrative platforms, and territorial assets within a Company-administered territory shall remain the property of the Company or of a Company-controlled holding entity, unless a written instrument approved by the Governor expressly provides otherwise.

### 2. No Private Estate in Company Territory

No Resident, Villager, Chartered Merchant, Visitor, Contractor, or user acquires ownership of Company territorial property merely by residence, use, payment of fees, possession of a token, participation in a platform, or duration of occupancy.

### 3. Tokenized License

The Company may issue a digital token, credential, smart-contract record, or registry entry representing a contractual license to occupy, access, use, trade from, or administer a defined area, dwelling, stall, berth, warehouse, commercial unit, digital space, or service entitlement.

### 4. Nature of the Token

A token issued under this Article is an administrative and contractual license credential only. It is not:

1. a deed;
2. a freehold estate;
3. a sovereign grant;
4. a voting right;
5. a share of the Company;
6. a security, unless applicable law determines otherwise;
7. a tax instrument;
8. a right to govern the Company;
9. a right to exclude public authorities acting under host-state law.

### 5. License Agreement Required

Every tokenized territorial right shall be governed by a written license agreement, occupancy agreement, merchant agreement, platform agreement, or Local Charter provision stating:

1. the area or service covered;
2. the permitted use;
3. the duration of the license;
4. fees and renewal terms;
5. transferability or non-transferability;
6. grounds for suspension or termination;
7. dispute process;
8. relationship to host-state law;
9. whether the license may be revoked, renewed, assigned, inherited, pledged, or sold.

### 6. Host-Law Override

If host-state law treats any tokenized license, occupancy agreement, dwelling arrangement, merchant agreement, or access credential as a lease, tenancy, concession, consumer contract, security, payment instrument, or regulated financial product, host-state law controls.

The Company shall structure and administer such tokens only within the limits of applicable law.

### 7. Dwelling Protection Preserved

Nothing in this Article weakens the Dwelling and Essential-Services Guarantee. A Resident's token may be suspended or restricted for lawful cause, but access to a dwelling, essential utilities, and lawful entry and exit may be limited only as permitted by host-state law and this Code.

### 8. Private Movable Property

Company ownership of territorial land and infrastructure does not extend to the lawful personal property, tools, inventory, goods, documents, clothing, devices, or private effects of Residents, Villagers, Chartered Merchants, Visitors, Contractors, or users.

### 9. No Confiscation by Token Failure

Loss, expiry, suspension, burn, or technical failure of a token does not transfer a person's lawful private movable property to the Company.

Any seizure, lien, storage, abandonment, or disposal of private property must follow written rules, due process, and applicable host-state law.

### 10. Registry Supremacy

The Company registry shall be the authoritative internal record of tokenized licenses, but it shall not override host-state land registries, corporate registries, court orders, public records, or mandatory legal filings.

## Article 6 — Voluntary Association

No person shall be compelled to become a Resident, Villager, Chartered Merchant, or user of Company services.

All membership shall be voluntary and contractual.

### Absolute Exit Right

Every person has the unconditional right to physically depart any Company-administered territory at any time.

Departure shall never be conditioned on payment of debts, completion of procedures, surrender of property lawfully owned by the departing person, or Company consent.

Outstanding debts and contractual obligations survive departure as ordinary civil claims, enforceable only through lawful process.

Safety procedures, such as checkpoint screening on exit from hazardous or restricted facilities, may regulate the manner of departure but never its availability.

# Book III — Governing Authorities

## Article 7 — The Governor

The Governor is the supreme internal authority of the Company. Nothing in this Code transfers final control of the Company from the Governor.

The Governor may adopt, amend, repeal, suspend, interpret, or ratify:

1. this Code, subject to amendment limits;
2. operating bylaws;
3. territorial regulations;
4. digital-platform rules;
5. merchant regulations;
6. treasury rules;
7. arbitration rules;
8. emergency directives;
9. compliance policies.

The Governor's authority is internal and corporate. It does not override host-state law.

The Governor may delegate authority in writing but may not permanently surrender final interpretive authority unless the Company is formally reorganized.

Constraints in this Code on the manner and timing of the Governor's powers exist to make the Company's commitments credible to residents, clients, and host governments. They limit how power is exercised, not who holds it.

## Article 7A — Succession and Incapacity

The Governor shall maintain a written, dated instrument of succession designating one or more successors in order of priority, lodged with the Legal Representative and the Office of Compliance and Legality.

Upon the Governor's death, legal incapacity, or written resignation, the designated successor assumes the office of Governor with full authority under this Code.

If no valid succession instrument exists, the Legal Representative shall serve as Acting Governor with all powers except amendment of the Entrenched Articles, and shall convene the Court of Directors within 30 days to identify a permanent Governor in accordance with the Company's corporate constitution and applicable corporate law.

A dispute over succession shall be resolved under the corporate law of the Company's jurisdiction of incorporation.

During any such dispute, no party may amend this Code, move the treasury beyond ordinary operations, or alter resident rights.

## Article 7B — Proprietary Governance; Prohibition of Electoral Mechanisms

The Company is governed proprietarily, not democratically.

Authority within the Company flows from ownership, appointment, and contract — never from election, vote, referendum, or majority will of Residents, Villagers, Chartered Merchants, users, or any other class of persons.

Within Company governance and within every Company-administered territory, platform, and instrument:

1. no Company office, including Governor, Director, Territorial Administrator, or tribunal officer, is or may be made elective;
2. no class of persons holds, or may acquire by any means, voting rights, electoral rights, initiative rights, recall rights, or referendum rights over the Company, this Code, any Local Charter, any rule, any fee, or any officer;
3. no Local Charter, platform rule, merchant regulation, token design, smart contract, or delegated instrument may introduce, simulate, or evolve toward an electoral, plebiscitary, or majority-rule governance mechanism;
4. no petition, poll, survey, assembly, comment process, or governance-comment instrument creates any binding effect; all such mechanisms are consultative only, and the Governor's decision following any consultation is final;
5. tokens shall never confer governance votes, and any token, credential, or contract term purporting to do so is void from inception.

No practice, custom, course of dealing, expectation, or passage of time shall ripen into a democratic or electoral right within the Company.

This Article governs the Company's own constitution and instruments only. It does not restrict any person's civic or political rights under host-state law, including voting in public elections, political speech, association, petitioning public authorities, or criticizing the Company.

Where a host government conditions a delegation or special-zone authorization on participatory mechanisms, the Company may administer such mechanisms as a service to the host authority, but such mechanisms bind only to the extent host law requires and confer no rights over the Company itself.

## Article 8 — The Court of Directors

The Company may maintain a Court of Directors.

The Court of Directors advises the Governor on:

1. trade;
2. finance;
3. territorial administration;
4. legal compliance;
5. digital assets;
6. infrastructure;
7. security;
8. relations with host governments;
9. resident welfare;
10. dispute systems.

The Court of Directors has no power to override the Governor unless expressly granted by this Code.

For any amendment of an Entrenched Article, any token issuance, or any treasury movement above the Material Threshold, the Governor shall obtain and preserve the Court's written advice before acting.

The Governor may act against that advice, but the advice and the Governor's written reasons become part of the Company record available to auditors and, where contractually promised, to affected stakeholders.

## Article 9 — Territorial Administrators

Each Company-administered territory may have a Territorial Administrator.

The Administrator is responsible for:

1. local service delivery;
2. registry operations;
3. utilities coordination;
4. local rule enforcement;
5. resident onboarding;
6. emergency management;
7. community relations;
8. reporting to the Governor.

The Administrator acts as an officer of a private governance-services provider, not as a governor of a sovereign territory.

## Article 10 — Office of Compliance and Legality

The Company shall maintain an Office of Compliance and Legality.

This office shall oversee:

1. host-law compliance;
2. anti-money-laundering controls;
3. sanctions compliance;
4. securities-law review;
5. data-protection obligations;
6. consumer protection;
7. tax reporting;
8. labor compliance;
9. financial licensing;
10. environmental compliance.

No digital token, treasury system, financial service, or trading program may launch without internal compliance review.

For any token offered to persons outside the Company, written sign-off from qualified external legal counsel is required in each jurisdiction of offer addressing securities, payments, and money-services licensing.

The Company shall not operate payment, settlement, custody, or money-transmission services in any jurisdiction without the license or registration that jurisdiction requires, or a written external legal opinion that none is required.

## Article 10A — The Ombudsman

The Company shall retain an independent Ombudsman who is not an employee of the Company and whose compensation is fixed in advance for a defined term.

The Ombudsman may receive complaints from any Resident, Villager, Chartered Merchant, or Credentialed Observer; investigate with access to relevant Company records; and issue written, non-binding recommendations to the Governor.

The Governor retains full authority to accept or reject any recommendation, but rejections of recommendations concerning Entrenched Articles shall be in writing and preserved in the Company record.

No person shall be penalized for raising a complaint with the Ombudsman in good faith.

# Book IV — Rights and Duties

## Article 11 — Company Bill of Rights

Every Resident, Villager, Chartered Merchant, and lawful user shall have the following internal rights:

1. right to written notice of binding rules;
2. right to contractual clarity;
3. right to due process before non-emergency expulsion;
4. right to appeal suspension or expulsion;
5. right to protection of lawful private movable property, deposits, refunds, contractual entitlements, and tokenized license interests, subject to this Code and host-state law;
6. right to privacy and data protection;
7. right to fair access to dispute resolution, including independent arbitration;
8. right to leave the community under Article 6;
9. right to non-discrimination under applicable law;
10. right to be free from forced labor, coercion, unlawful detention, or physical abuse;
11. right of Residents to dwelling and essential services under Article 5;
12. right to receive wages, refunds, and deposit returns in lawful money of the host state, never solely in internal Company units.

## Article 12 — Duties of Members

Every Resident, Villager, Chartered Merchant, and user shall:

1. obey applicable host-state law;
2. obey Company rules;
3. respect the rights of others;
4. avoid damaging Company infrastructure;
5. avoid malware, unlawful data, fraud, theft, harassment, or unauthorized access;
6. comply with identity, safety, and financial rules;
7. pay lawful fees, rents, charges, or dues owed under contract;
8. cooperate with lawful investigations by the Company or public authorities.

## Article 13 — Due Process

No person shall be permanently expelled from a Company-administered territory without:

1. written notice;
2. stated reasons;
3. opportunity to respond;
4. review by a designated officer, tribunal, or independent arbitrator as the dispute-resolution rules require.

Emergency suspension may occur first where delay creates a serious risk.

Every emergency suspension shall be reviewed:

1. within 72 hours, where the suspension affects residency privileges, dwelling-adjacent services, or a person's primary livelihood within the territory;
2. within 14 days, for platform-only, merchant-privilege, or facility-access suspensions.

A suspension not reviewed within the applicable period lapses automatically.

Removal of a Resident from a dwelling remains governed exclusively by Article 5.

# Book V — Territorial Governance Services

## Article 14 — Governance as a Service

The Company may provide governance services to:

1. private land developments;
2. special economic zones;
3. charter cities;
4. private ports;
5. digital communities;
6. research parks;
7. trade corridors;
8. industrial zones;
9. freeports;
10. private settlements;
11. host-government pilot jurisdictions.

Governance services may include:

1. registries;
2. permitting;
3. licensing;
4. arbitration administration;
5. utility coordination;
6. compliance systems;
7. resident identity;
8. property records;
9. merchant registration;
10. commercial rules;
11. digital payments infrastructure;
12. treasury services;
13. emergency coordination;
14. public-facing service portals.

All such services shall be based on contract, lawful delegation, property rights, or host-jurisdiction authorization.

Every host-government delegation or special-zone authorization shall be paired with a written continuity plan addressing repeal, non-renewal, or invalidation of the delegation, including protection of resident records, deposits, and exit rights.

## Article 15 — Fees, Not Taxes

The Company may charge:

1. service fees;
2. rents;
3. platform fees;
4. licensing fees;
5. maintenance assessments;
6. merchant dues;
7. treasury contributions;
8. infrastructure charges.

The Company shall not describe private fees as sovereign taxes unless a host government has lawfully delegated tax administration authority.

Fee schedules shall be published.

Fees applicable to an existing Resident or Chartered Merchant may be increased only:

1. as the person's contract expressly provides; or
2. on no less than 60 days' published notice, taking effect at the next renewal term unless the contract provides otherwise.

Emergency cost-recovery surcharges require Court of Directors advice on record and lapse after 12 months unless re-adopted.

## Article 16 — Local Charters

Each territory administered by the Company shall have a Local Charter containing:

1. name of the territory;
2. legal basis of Company authority;
3. land title, lease, concession, contract, or license basis;
4. ownership status of all territorial land and infrastructure;
5. tokenized license classes;
6. rules for occupancy, access, transfer, renewal, suspension, and termination of tokenized licenses;
7. resident categories;
8. service fees;
9. dispute process;
10. registry system;
11. infrastructure rules;
12. emergency powers;
13. exit rights;
14. relationship to host-state law;
15. amendment procedure.

No Local Charter may derogate from the Entrenched Articles.

# Book VI — Trade, Merchants, and Digital Commerce

## Article 17 — Chartered Merchants

The Company may license Chartered Merchants to conduct lawful trade, commerce, logistics, digital services, financial services, research, construction, infrastructure support, professional services, manufacturing, warehousing, port services, or marketplace activity within Company-administered systems or territory.

A Chartered Merchant license is a contractual privilege, not a sovereign franchise, public monopoly, voting right, or property title.

Every Chartered Merchant shall operate under:

1. applicable host-state law;
2. this Code;
3. the relevant Local Charter;
4. merchant regulations;
5. the merchant's written license agreement;
6. applicable compliance, consumer-protection, tax, labor, and financial rules.

The Company may suspend or revoke a Chartered Merchant license for fraud, non-payment, breach of safety rules, sanctions violations, consumer harm, unlawful trade, money-laundering risk, environmental harm, repeated breach of Company rules, or other written cause.

Suspension or revocation is subject to notice, reasons, and review, except where emergency action is required.

## Article 18 — Tokens and Digital Assets

The Company may issue digital tokens, credentials, registry entries, smart-contract records, access passes, merchant credentials, occupancy-license credentials, loyalty units, payment tokens, utility tokens, or treasury instruments only where lawful.

No token shall confer voting power, democratic governance power, sovereignty, citizenship, tax authority, criminal authority, land ownership, or public office.

A token may represent:

1. a license to access a defined area;
2. a license to occupy a dwelling or commercial unit;
3. a license to trade from a defined location;
4. a digital identity credential;
5. a service entitlement;
6. a merchant credential;
7. a treasury or payment function, where legally permitted;
8. a registry record;
9. a contractual entitlement.

Every token design shall be reviewed by the Office of Compliance and Legality before launch.

External legal sign-off is required where tokens may be securities, payment instruments, money-services products, consumer financial products, or regulated digital assets.

## Article 19 — Treasury and Material Threshold

The Company may maintain treasury systems for lawful operations, infrastructure, reserves, emergency response, digital commerce, and resident-service continuity.

Treasury rules shall define:

1. custody standards;
2. signatory authority;
3. audit procedures;
4. reserve requirements;
5. spending approvals;
6. conflict-of-interest rules;
7. material movement thresholds;
8. emergency treasury powers;
9. reporting duties.

Any treasury movement above the Material Threshold requires Court of Directors advice on record before execution unless emergency action is required to prevent imminent loss, legal breach, infrastructure failure, or resident harm.

Emergency treasury action shall be documented and reviewed after the fact.

# Book VII — Dispute Resolution and Arbitration

## Article 20 — Dispute Resolution

The Company shall provide a fair dispute-resolution process for Residents, Villagers, Chartered Merchants, Contractors, Credentialed Observers, and lawful users.

Dispute resolution may include:

1. internal review;
2. administrative reconsideration;
3. Ombudsman complaint;
4. mediation;
5. independent arbitration;
6. host-state court process where required by law.

No dispute-resolution clause may unlawfully deprive a person of mandatory rights under host-state law.

Disputes involving dwelling removal, unlawful detention, criminal allegations, public authority action, or mandatory consumer or employment protections remain subject to host-state law.

## Article 21 — Enforcement Limits

The Company may enforce private rules through contractual remedies, access restrictions, license suspension, fines where contractually authorized, merchant-license action, platform restrictions, arbitration claims, civil claims, and lawful cooperation with public authorities.

The Company shall not use unlawful force, private imprisonment, forced labor, confiscation without process, or coercive detention.

Physical security personnel, if used, shall operate only within host-state law, licensing rules, use-of-force rules, and written Company policy.

# Book VIII — Security, Emergency Powers, and Public Authorities

## Article 22 — Security Services

The Company may provide or contract for lawful security services to protect persons, infrastructure, digital systems, common areas, facilities, and Company assets.

Security services are private protective services only. They are not military authority, sovereign policing, or criminal jurisdiction unless expressly delegated by host-state law.

Security rules shall preserve:

1. lawful exit rights;
2. resident dwelling protections;
3. access to essential services;
4. cooperation with public authorities;
5. complaint and review procedures.

## Article 23 — Emergency Powers

The Governor or authorized officer may issue emergency directives where necessary to address serious risks to safety, infrastructure, systems, legal compliance, public order, environmental protection, or continuity of essential services.

Emergency directives must be:

1. written where practicable;
2. time-limited;
3. proportionate;
4. reviewable;
5. recorded in Company records.

Emergency powers may not override the Absolute Exit Right or the Dwelling and Essential-Services Guarantee.

## Article 24 — Records

The Company shall maintain records sufficient to support:

1. legal compliance;
2. financial accountability;
3. resident rights;
4. merchant licensing;
5. tokenized license registries;
6. dispute resolution;
7. treasury review;
8. succession continuity;

Records shall be retained according to written policy and applicable law.

## Article 25 — Transparency

The Company may publish charters, fee schedules, rules, audit summaries, service-level summaries, compliance notices, and governance records where appropriate.

Transparency shall be balanced against privacy, security, trade secrets, legal privilege, and contractual confidentiality.

Credentialed Observers may receive defined access under written credentials.

# Book X — Amendment, Entrenchment, and Continuity

## Article 26 — Amendment Power

The Governor may amend this Code, subject to host-state law, corporate law, contract obligations, and the Entrenched Articles.

Amendments shall be written, dated, preserved in Company records, and published where they affect Residents, Villagers, Chartered Merchants, Contractors, or lawful users.

## Article 27 — Entrenched Articles

The following principles are entrenched and may not be weakened by Local Charter, platform rule, merchant regulation, treasury rule, token design, emergency directive, or subordinate instrument:

1. host-state law override;
2. non-sovereignty;
3. voluntary association;
4. absolute physical exit right;
5. dwelling and essential-services guarantee;
6. due process for suspension and expulsion;
7. non-democratic proprietary governance;
8. prohibition on token voting;
9. protection against forced labor, unlawful detention, coercion, and physical abuse;
10. protection of lawful private movable property;
11. requirement that tokenized territorial rights remain licenses unless host-state law and approved written instruments provide otherwise;
12. compliance review for tokens, treasury systems, and regulated financial activity.

## Article 28 — Continuity Planning

The Company shall maintain continuity plans for:

1. political reversal;
2. host-government non-renewal;
3. delegation repeal;
4. infrastructure disruption;
5. cyber failure;
6. treasury access failure;
7. death or incapacity of the Governor;
8. resident record protection;
9. deposit and refund protection;
10. orderly exit or transition.

# Book XI — Final Clauses

## Article 29 — Interpretation

This Code shall be interpreted to preserve the Company's private, contractual, proprietary, host-law-subordinate, and non-sovereign character.

Where any provision may be read as either a lawful private governance-services rule or an unlawful claim of sovereign authority, the lawful private governance-services reading shall prevail.

Where any provision conflicts with host-state law, host-state law controls.

## Article 30 — Operating Principle

The Central American Trading Company is not selling pieces of territory.

It is operating a proprietary jurisdictional platform where land-use, occupancy, commercial, digital, and access rights are licensed through tokens under contract, subject always to host-state law.

The Company's promises are to be treated as institutional capital. Its credibility depends on lawful operation, voluntary association, resident protection, contractual clarity, compliance discipline, and the Governor's willingness to bind power to written rules.
